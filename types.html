  • Great White Shark
    Great white sharks are one of the most well-known predators in the ocean. They can grow up to 20 feet long and weigh over 4,000 pounds. These sharks have sharp, triangular teeth and a powerful sense of smell, which helps them hunt seals, fish, and even smaller sharks. Great whites are found in oceans around the world, but they prefer cooler waters near coastlines.
  • Whale Shark
    Whale sharks are the largest fish in the world, growing up to 40 feet long or more. Despite their size, they are gentle giants that feed mostly on plankton, small fish, and krill by filtering water through their mouths. Whale sharks live in tropical and warm oceans, and they are often seen near the surface. People travel from all over the world to swim with these peaceful creatures.
  • Hammerhead Shark
    Hammerhead sharks are known for their unusual head shape, which looks like a hammer. This unique design helps them see better and hunt prey like fish, squid, and stingrays. Hammerheads are usually about 10-15 feet long and can be found in warm, coastal waters around the globe. Some species of hammerhead sharks like to swim in schools, especially during the day.
